For regular carbon steel or stainless-steel welding wires, the wire feed tube and wire feed wheels that come with the machine are suitable.

But when it comes to welding aluminum wires, we suggest using ER5356-grade aluminum wire. This type of wire is a bit harder, which reduces the likelihood of wire jams and deformation.

Additionally, a graphene-lined wire feed tube, like the one mentioned, has a graphite inner layer that can significantly reduce friction, further minimizing the risk of wire jams.

If you're concerned about wire jams when welding aluminum, I'd suggest that once the machine arrives, you can first give it a try with the standard setup. We do have ER5356 aluminum wire available on our website: Premium Aluminum Welding Wire

As for the graphene-lined wire feed tube, our standard configuration comes with a regular tube. You can start with the regular one and if you experience wire-jamming issues, then consider purchasing a graphene-lined one.
